%     \item \textbf{Conductivity output range.} The conductivity network outputs a single scalar field $\gamma^{\mathrm{NN}}(x,y)$, common to all boundary conditions. Its final output is reparametrized through a sigmoid and then rescaled to a prescribed interval $[\gamma_{\min},\gamma_{\max}]$. The presence of this rescaling is physically motivated; the conductivity cannot be zero, thus the presence of $\gamma_\text{min}$. Further, we assume we are not working with superconductors, so we set an upper bound $\gamma_\text{max}$. This value is, however, much larger than the maximum true value, to ensure that the recovery is not affected by the saturation of this bound. In the experiments considered here, $\gamma_{\min}=0.5$, while $\gamma_{\max}$ is chosen depending on the conductivity profile under consideration, rather than being fixed universally across all runs.
